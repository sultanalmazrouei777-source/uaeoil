Adult Zone

Oil in the UAE ‚Äì A Deeper Look

How did the discovery of oil transform the UAE, and where can you explore more?

From Pearls and Trading Ports to Oil Producers

Before oil, the emirates along the southern Gulf coast relied on fishing, pearling, small-scale trade and oasis agriculture. Towns such as Abu Dhabi and Dubai were modest settlements clustered around creeks or coastal inlets. Life was shaped by the sea, the desert and seasonal pearl-diving campaigns.

In the early 20th century, foreign oil companies began conducting geological surveys and signing concessions. Exploratory drilling in Abu Dhabi started around 1950. Some of the first wells were dry, but by the late 1950s, commercial quantities of oil had been discovered both offshore at Umm Shaif and onshore in the Bab field. In 1962, Abu Dhabi exported its first cargo of crude oil, marking the start of a new era.

Building a New State with Oil Revenues

Oil income provided the capital needed to modernise quickly. Under leaders such as Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, revenue was channelled into:

  • Roads, ports and airports linking previously isolated communities.
  • Electricity, water and housing programmes that improved daily life.
  • Free education, scholarships and universities for citizens.
  • Hospitals, clinics and preventative healthcare.

When the United Arab Emirates was formed in 1971, oil revenues helped the new federation invest in common infrastructure while also supporting each emirate‚Äôs development priorities. Over time, the UAE used oil as a springboard to build a more diversified economy based on trade, logistics, finance, tourism, technology and cultural initiatives.

Work and Technology in the Oil Fields

Development of onshore and offshore oil fields required large teams of engineers, geologists, drillers, mechanics, safety specialists and support staff. Many worked long shifts in harsh desert conditions or on offshore platforms, far from city centres. Their efforts, alongside Emirati leadership and planning, turned the UAE into a major energy producer.

Over the decades, technology improved recovery rates and safety, while national companies gained more control and expertise. At the same time, the UAE began thinking about the long-term need to balance oil with renewable energy, advanced industries and a knowledge-based economy.
